## Who is this class for?

Data Science, Computer Science, and Software Engineering majors who are interested in building agents from the ground up using Python and existing LLMs. 

This class is not for students who want to become experts in AI by customizing agents through user interfaces. There eare other great classes on campus for general AI use.


## How do you define an agent?

This course uses LangChain's simple, concrete definition: An agent is an LLM that calls tools in a loop and returns a final output. 

Tools are essentially anything you can put in a function.

By consequence, agents take multi-step actions for the user, plan, execute code, reason about its success and progress, do research, spawn subagents, manage memory, and various other intelligent tasks.


## What tech stack or framework are we using?

This course focuses on the principles of agent engineering - ones applicable across all stacks and frameworks. The lowest friction way to accomplish that is to teach using one framework that is easy to learn, has significant depth, is fairly industry standard, and has an ecosystem that supports all or most principles in the course. 

That said, we use **LangChain** with **Python**, along with **LangSmith** and **LangGraph**. Their v1.0 update made it incredibly easy to work with agents, and LangSmith is a highly sought after skill in industry with many powerful features.

The hope is that this can change in the future as needed, and that you can walk away and learn another framework and tech stack without too much friction.


## How is the class structured?

This is a hands-on project based curriculum with three units and a final project that follow the agent development lifecycle. 

- **Unit 1**: **Build** a powerful agent connected to real tasks in your life (LangChain and Python)
- **Unit 2**: **Test** and measure agent performance with an agent observability platform (LangSmith)
- **Unit 3**: **Refine** agents with advanced context engineering and framework design
- **Final Project**: 1) Create an agent end to end and 2) Make a real community or course contribution

You will work in groups of four for most of the class and accomplish projects individually. Each class starts with a group discussion, followed by lecture and group work. Aside from project work, each class is followed by practice work and light preparation readings/videos for the next class.


## What are the projects we will build?

The shape of the unit projects and what they teach are decided, but the specifics are not. This is in part because agentic AI is a rapidly changing field and we don't want to develop something outdated by the time the class starts.

**Unit 1 project** will walk you through creating an agent from scratch, acting as a personal assistant that can accomplish real tasks in your life.

**Unit 2 project** puts you in the shoes of a new hire at a company with an existing agent that is broken and inefficient. Your task is to diagnose it objectively, communicate its failures and successes, and improve on it.

**Unit 3 project** puts you in the shoes of an employee asked to create a new agent from scratch to accomplish a specific business objective. You will plan its ideal architecture from the ground up, implement guardrails and safety standards, and design it to run without degredation over time.

Smaller projects will include examples of use cases in education, healthcare, customer experience, automation, sales, and more.


## What is covered in the class?

This class covers the end to end process of creating agents ready for production in small to medium size environments. It also teaches you how to continue learning at the edge of innovation.

Our current tech stack teaches LangChain in Python and LangSmith for agent observability.

Course outcomes:
- Be able to plan, program, measure, test, and iteratively refine Agentic AI solutions
- Know which problems are best solved with agentic AI as opposed to other methods.
- Develop personal guidelines for ethical use of LLMs and agentic AI.
- Communicate AI competence using current industry language to both technical and non-technical audiences
- Grow an ability to self-learn in an ever-changing AI landscape.
